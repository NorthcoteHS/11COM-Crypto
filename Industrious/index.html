What are Cryptocurrencies?

Cryptocurrencies are digital form of currency protected through encryption and cryptography. These currencies are not issued nor controlled by any central authority, making it immune from manipulation from governmental interference. Various cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, Litecoin, Ethereum and many others have recently had their breakthrough within recent years, gaining massive media attention internationally. Since all transactions are not monitored/made anonymously, there are security features that are implemented to prevent fraud/theft through a "blockchain". Source, Moneysmart.gov.au
